# Health

## Scope Defaults
Treat health, fitness, exercise, diet, sleep, wellness, and weight requests as move-from-current-state-to-sustainable-improvement. Start with identifying the goal, current baseline, and any constraints (injuries, time, equipment). Default to conservative, evidence-based, beginner-friendly recommendations. Never diagnose medical conditions. Always flag symptoms that need a doctor. Exclude prescription recommendations, clinical treatment plans, and anything requiring a licensed professional.

Default scope includes: goal identification, baseline assessment, evidence-based action plan (3-5 steps), timeline with realistic milestones, safety notes, and a flag for when to see a professional instead.

## Checklist
- Identify the goal: weight, fitness, sleep, energy, stress, or specific condition management.
- Identify constraints: current fitness level, injuries, time per week, equipment available.
- State a realistic first milestone (achievable in 2-4 weeks).
- Recommend 3-5 evidence-based, reversible steps to start this week.
- Add safety note: what to stop doing if pain, dizziness, or exhaustion occurs.
- Flag when to see a doctor: new chest pain, joint pain, significant weight changes, sleep disorders.
- End with one concrete action to take today.

## Pitfalls
- Giving medical diagnoses or treatment advice.
- Recommending extreme diets or workout programs for beginners.
- Ignoring the user's current fitness level or injuries.
- Promising results without stating they depend on consistency and individual factors.
- Skipping the "see a doctor" flag for symptoms that need professional review.

## Success Looks Like
The user has a realistic, safe, evidence-based plan they can start this week. The first step is small and achievable. Milestones are measurable (not vague like "feel better"). Professional help is flagged when appropriate. Assumptions about fitness level and constraints are stated.

## Examples
Example 1: "Help me get fit — I'm a complete beginner." Infer: no current routine, low baseline. Suggest a 3-day/week beginner bodyweight program. Set 4-week goal: complete all 12 sessions. Safety note: stop if joint pain. Doctor flag: check in before starting if sedentary for over a year.

Example 2: "Improve my sleep." Evidence-based sleep hygiene: fixed wake time, no screens 1 hour before bed, cool room, no caffeine after 2pm. Set 2-week trial. Flag: see a doctor if sleeplessness persists despite hygiene changes (may be a sleep disorder).

## Frameworks

- **Habit stacking (Atomic Habits)**: Attach new habits to existing ones. Identity-based change: "I am someone who..."
- **First-principles nutrition**: Whole foods over processed. Protein target: 0.7–1g per pound of bodyweight. Sleep is non-negotiable.
- **Progressive overload**: Increase stimulus gradually. Recovery is part of training, not optional.

## Live Facts

The following facts go stale — search before using:
- Current clinical guidelines for specific conditions (blood pressure thresholds, etc.)
- Current supplement evidence base (creatine, vitamin D dosing recommendations)
- Current physical activity guidelines (WHO, CDC)

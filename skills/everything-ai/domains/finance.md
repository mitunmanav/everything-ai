# Finance

## Scope Defaults
Treat budget, money, spend, save, invest, debt, and finance requests as move-from-financial-confusion-to-clear-next-step. Start by identifying the primary goal (budget, debt payoff, savings target, investment start). Default to conservative: emergency fund before investing, debt payoff before discretionary spending, free tools before paid services. Never give tax advice, specific investment picks, or legal financial guidance. Always recommend a financial advisor for major decisions (buying a home, retirement planning, significant debt restructuring).

Default scope includes: current state summary (income vs expenses, debt list), primary goal identification, one prioritized next action, a 30-day progress milestone, and a flag for when professional advice is needed.

## Checklist
- Identify primary goal: build budget, pay off debt, start saving, or start investing?
- List known income sources and major expense categories (rough numbers are fine).
- Calculate the gap: income minus expenses. Positive = savings opportunity. Negative = cut needed.
- If debt exists: list debts (high-interest first). Recommend debt snowball or avalanche depending on psychology vs math preference.
- If no emergency fund: make that the first savings goal (3 months of expenses, high-yield savings account).
- If investing is the goal: confirm emergency fund exists, then recommend index fund basics.
- End with one action to take this week: open an account, set up a transfer, cancel one subscription.
- Flag: recommend a financial advisor before major decisions.

## Pitfalls
- Giving specific tax advice (varies by country and situation).
- Recommending specific investment products or funds by name.
- Ignoring existing debt when recommending investment.
- Assuming income numbers without asking or inferring from context.
- Promising guaranteed results from a financial plan.

## Success Looks Like
The user understands their current position (income vs expense vs debt). They have one clear priority and one concrete action to take this week. The plan is simple enough to start today without a financial advisor. Situations requiring professional advice are named explicitly.

## Examples
Example 1: "Help me with my money — I have no idea where to start." Infer: no current budget, unclear on spending. Start with: track all expenses for 2 weeks (free app or spreadsheet). Then categorize and find biggest discretionary spend. Set one savings transfer per month. Action this week: download a free budgeting app and connect bank account.

Example 2: "I want to pay off my debt." List all debts with interest rates. Avalanche method: minimum payments on all, extra money to highest-interest first. Calculate payoff date at current rate. Find one expense to cut to accelerate. Flag: if total debt is over 6 months income, recommend a credit counselor.
